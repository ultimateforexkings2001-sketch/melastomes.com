Research & Further Reading

Melastomataceae is an actively studied family, with ongoing taxonomic and systematics work happening at herbaria and universities worldwide. This page collects where to look for peer-reviewed, primary research — the kind of source that goes well beyond what a reference site like this one can responsibly cover.

Where the real taxonomic work happens

Formal species descriptions, phylogenetic studies, and revisions of Melastomataceae genera are published in botanical journals and maintained through herbarium databases rather than general web content. University herbaria — including long-running collections like the University of Florida Herbarium — maintain the reference specimens that this kind of research depends on.

How this site fits in

Melastomes.com is a general-audience reference and growing guide — useful for identifying a genus, understanding basic taxonomy, or learning how to care for a melastome as a houseplant. It isn't a substitute for peer-reviewed literature, and we link out to primary sources wherever it's helpful rather than restating research we can't independently verify.

A note on collaboration

Botanical reference projects like this one benefit enormously from researchers and enthusiasts who are willing to share expertise, correct errors, and point toward better sources. If you work in this field and notice something on this site that should be corrected or expanded, we'd genuinely like to hear from you.
